What is Spectrophotometry?

Spectrophotometry is a method to measure the intensity of light at different wavelengths as it passes through a sample. This can be used to analyze the composition and concentration of substances in the sample.

Basic Principle:

Different light sources emit light at specific wavelengths. By measuring the intensity across multiple wavelengths, we can characterize the light source and later use it to analyze samples.
